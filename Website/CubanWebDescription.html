Weather Based Cuban Link

Overview

Figure 1: Represents the entire necklace

The Weather Based Cuban Link is a jewelry chain that can light up different colors based on the current weather.
The link will pull weather data from an accompanying app, and throughout the day individual pixels/lights will light up, eventually forming a full display of the day at midnight and noon.
The chain will be able to support multiple modes that can be changed through the app.
Currently the planned modes include weather clock, customized color setting, and a real time color update based on the weather.
The weather clock mode mode is a mode that will incrementally light up chains on the link as the day progresses, and at midnight the chain will shine brightest with all of the colors recorded throughout the day on a respective link.
The custom color mode will be a color picker wheel that users can manipulate to make their chain shine in whatever color they choose.
The lights will be able to be made brighter and dimmer through the color picker as well.
The real time weather mode will work much like a mood ring that reacts to the weather.
Depending on the current weather, the chain will flow through colors that represent the current climate in the area.
Please refer to our project proposal for all of the materials and their expected costs.

Hardware:

Arduino

The largest piece of hardware that will be introduced into our chain is the Arduino Nano 33 BLE Sense.
This arduino has capabilities that include a 9 axis Inertial Measurement Unit, temperature, pressure, humidity, light, color and even gestures sensors, and a microphone.
Using these sensors, we would program the device to be able to support modes such as real real time weather mode and collect data that is relevant to the user.
Because the device is bluetooth connected, this is the mode of communication that we would use to connect to the device via phone/app.
Because the BLE was not able to supply 5v of power to our neopixel LEDs, we added a second arduino to our hardware. We have included a Arduino Uno that's sole purpose is to handle displaying and sending color information to our LEDs. The Arduino Uno recieves information about what colors to display from the Arduino BLE. This board to board communication is handled through master-slave I2C communication.

Neopixel Jewels

Neopixel jewels will be the main electronic light that will be used throughout the project.
Each link on the chain will contain a neopixel jewel, and will be run in series starting from the arduino at the back of the chain.
The arduino will power the lights when on, and will also be the medium for passing rgb values to the lights.

Software:

Our software will also be a large component of the project.
The app itself will be the hub that controls what mode the chain is in, as well as responding with the status of the chain such as battery, weather, and current light colors.
The app will be crucial for connecting over bluetooth, which is a main feature that must be implemented.
The app contains two screens for users to interact with.
The first screen is a home screen that includes the current mode of the cuban link, whether the cuban link is connected over bluetooth, the current RGB values on the cuban link, and the current temperature around the user.
